DECISION PRIORITY RULES
==================================================

Use these priority rules when a clause could fit more than one topic:

A. Specific feature beats general evaluation.
- “Beautiful beach and worth visiting” -> Topic 9, not Topic 10
- “Interesting museum and worth a visit” -> Topic 12, not Topic 10
- “Helpful staff and good visit” -> Topic 2, not Topic 10
- “Good local food and nice street” -> Topic 5, not Topic 10

B. Cost beats activity/service when money is central.
- “The jeep ride was too expensive” -> Topic 1
- “Tickets cost too much” -> Topic 1
- “Not worth the money” -> Topic 1

C. Service beats attraction mention when staff/operation is central.
- “The dunes were nice but the staff were rude” -> Topic 2
- “The show was good but service was bad” -> Topic 2
- “The office helped us arrange the tour” -> Topic 2

D. Activity beats general visit judgment when fun/recreation is central.
- “The ride was fun and worth trying” -> Topic 3
- “The water park was great for kids” -> Topic 3

E. Coastal tourism beats general landscape when beach/sea/island is central.
- “Beautiful beach and clear water” -> Topic 9
- “The sunrise on the island was amazing” -> Topic 9
- “Boat trip was beautiful” -> Topic 9

F. Environmental condition beats coastal scenery when cleanliness/pollution is central.
- “The beach was full of plastic trash” -> Topic 11
- “The water was dirty” -> Topic 11
- “Clean water and no trash” -> Topic 11

G. Tourist crowding beats general visitor response when crowd/tourist trap is central.
- “Too many tourists and very noisy” -> Topic 8
- “It felt like a tourist trap” -> Topic 8

H. Urban heritage, cultural attractions, and historical learning must be separated carefully.
- “Beautiful temple architecture” -> Topic 4
- “Historic palace in Ho Chi Minh City” -> Topic 7
- “Museum explains Vietnamese history” -> Topic 12
- “Old building worth a photo” -> Topic 7 if it is an urban landmark; Topic 10 if no heritage/urban context is clear

I. Visitor response is used for personal reaction when no other concrete topic dominates.
- “I liked it and would come back” -> Topic 6
- “We would not return” -> Topic 6
- But “I liked the beach” -> Topic 9
- But “I liked the museum information” -> Topic 12
- But “I liked the food” -> Topic 5
